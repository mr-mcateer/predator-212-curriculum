### **Timing Mark Alignment (CRITICAL)**

**File:** [05-Timing-Mark-Alignment.md](05-Timing-Mark-Alignment.md)

**Video Reference:** Mr. Hall Part 4 (15:24) - https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4sKIi20SJNI

**Why it matters:** If timing marks don't align, engine won't compress or start.

**What you'll learn:**
- How to photograph timing marks BEFORE you disassemble
- What correct alignment looks like
- How to verify alignment through a full engine rotation
- Troubleshooting if marks don't line up

**What to do:**
1. Read this guide completely
2. Watch the video's timing section
3. Photograph your engine's timing marks at the start of disassembly
4. Use those photos to verify alignment during reassembly

## üéì WHY THESE ARE CRITICAL

**Valve Lash Adjustment:**
- Incorrect gap = valves don't open/close properly
- Too tight = valves don't open at all
- Too loose = compression loss and poor performance
- This is the #1 cause of startup failure

**Timing Marks:**
- Misalignment = internal collision
- Bent valves
- Engine won't run
- Cannot be fixed without partial disassembly

**Torque Specifications:**
- Too tight = aluminum threads strip
- Too loose = components come apart
- Inconsistent = leaks and failures
- Use a torque wrench, not feel
