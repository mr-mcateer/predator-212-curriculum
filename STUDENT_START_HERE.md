## âš™ Critical Concepts You Need to Know

### **Torque (NOT just "tight")**

Don't guess. Torque wrench = precision.
- Too tight = broken threads (aluminum is delicate)
- Too loose = parts falling off during startup
- Correct = engine works, nothing breaks

[Reference: docs/05-Torque-Specifications.md](docs/05-Torque-Specifications.md)

### **Valve Lash (Open + Close gap)**

This controls when valves open and close.
- Too tight = valves don't fully open (engine weak)
- Too loose = valves don't fully close (compression loss)
- Perfect = engine purrs

This is why we emphasize it so much.

### **Timing Marks (Everything aligns here)**

Before you disassemble, photograph the timing marks.
When you reassemble, align them exactly the same way.
Engine timing controls everything.

### **Ring Gaps (Measured in thousandths of an inch)**

Piston rings must have exact gaps.
- Too tight = rings stick (bad)
- Too loose = compression loss (bad)
- Just right = engine works (good)
